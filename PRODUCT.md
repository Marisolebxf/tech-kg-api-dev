# Product

## Register

product

## Users

This is a knowledge graph platform prototype for product and engineering teams evaluating a data-processing and graph-service workflow. Primary users include operators who ingest data, analysts who inspect graph results, and reviewers who resolve conflicts during entity, relation, and property governance.

## Product Purpose

The product demonstrates an end-to-end knowledge graph workflow: data ingestion, cleaning, extraction, disambiguation, review, incremental updates, graph construction, query, and business service exposure. Success means the workflow is easy to inspect, the status of each stage is obvious, and the interface supports evaluation and presentation without confusing the user.

## Brand Personality

Clear, technical, confident, and trustworthy. The interface should feel structured and operational rather than decorative. It should communicate control, traceability, and reviewability.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style decoration, playful consumer UI, and overly experimental visuals. Avoid cluttered glass effects, heavy gradients without function, mismatched component styles, and anything that makes the workflow harder to scan. The interface should not look like a consumer dashboard or a generic SaaS landing page.

## Design Principles

- Make the workflow readable at a glance.
- Keep related states and steps visually contained.
- Favor familiar enterprise controls over novelty.
- Use color to signal state and hierarchy, not decoration.
- Preserve clear evidence, review, and audit cues throughout the flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Keep labels explicit, support keyboard interaction, and avoid information encoded by color alone. Motion should stay subtle and should not block comprehension.
